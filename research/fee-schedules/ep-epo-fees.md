## 2. Unitary Patent (UP) renewal fees

Administered by the EPO (single annual payment covering all participating UPC states). Adopted as the **"True TOP 4"** structure (Select Committee, 24 June 2015) — equivalent to the sum of national renewal fees in the four most-validated states. ## 4. Notes — discounts, recent changes, surprises

- **Rule 6 / Rule 7a EPC fee reductions** apply for:
  - **Language reduction (Rule 6(3) EPC)** — 30% reduction on the filing fee and the examination fee for applicants who file in an admissible non-EPO official language (one of the official languages of an EPC contracting state, other than EN/FR/DE) and provide the translation into an EPO official language. Available **only** to natural persons, SMEs, universities, non-profit organisations, public research organisations and non-profit research orgs (Rule 6(4) EPC).
  - **Micro-entity reduction (Rule 7a EPC, in force since 1 Apr 2024)** — additional **30% reduction** on filing fee, search fee, examination fee, designation fee, grant fee, and renewal fees during pendency, available to **applicants who filed fewer than five European patent applications in the preceding five years** and qualify as natural persons, SMEs, universities, non-profit organisations, public research organisations or non-profit research organisations. Stackable with Rule 6.
  - See <https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r6.html> and <https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r7a.html>.
- **Filing/opposition/appeal fees frozen for 1 Apr 2026 adjustment** — only procedural fees (search/examination/designation/grant/renewals) increased; an unusual carve-out worth flagging in any cost model.
- **Surprise / watch-item:** The 2024 schedule **decreased** the 6th-year renewal fee briefly (from €1,180 to €1,155 in the original CA/D 4/22 path, then to €1,535 after restructure) — the renewal curve was steepened in 2024 to recover revenue formerly captured by validation. Models built on pre-2024 curves materially undercount mid-life maintenance cost.
- **UP vs. classical EP**: A Unitary Patent is cheaper than ~3-state validation through year ~10 and substantially cheaper than 4+ states for the full 20-year life — but **UP fees are not subject to Rule 6 / Rule 7a reductions**; reductions apply only to EPC application-phase fees, not to UP post-grant renewals.
- All amounts above are EUR and exclude attorney/agent fees, translation costs (Article 65 EPC validation), and national renewal fees post-grant for non-UP routes.
